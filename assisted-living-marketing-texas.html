The Texas senior care market — what operators need to know

Texas is simultaneously one of the most opportunity-rich and most challenging states for assisted living operators. The senior population is growing at 40% by 2030 — faster than almost anywhere in the country — driven by in-migration from California and the Northeast, a large existing retiree base, and rapidly aging Baby Boomers across all four major metros.

The challenge is that digital competition in Texas senior care has intensified significantly in the past three years. National referral aggregators, well-funded regional chains, and private equity-backed operators are all competing for the same family searches. Independent and mid-size operators who build direct marketing channels now will own their local markets before that window closes.

Texas senior care markets — by opportunity

Houston & Harris County
Largest market · High volume
Houston is the largest senior care market in Texas. Diverse population requires multilingual targeting — English and Spanish campaigns essential. Strong MA referral network through Houston Methodist, Memorial Hermann, and HCA Healthcare systems. CPL runs $55–$110.
Dallas-Fort Worth Metroplex
Most competitive · High conversion
DFW has the highest concentration of memory care facilities in Texas and the most competitive Google Ads market. Premium positioning and strong review volume critical. Baylor Scott & White and Texas Health Resources referral networks valuable. CPL runs $60–$120.
San Antonio & Bexar County
Strong military demographic
San Antonio has a significant military retiree population — VA-connected seniors aging into assisted living. University Health and CHRISTUS referral networks active. Lower digital competition than Houston or Dallas. CPL runs $40–$85. Spanish-language targeting important.
Austin & Central Texas
Fastest growing · Tech-savvy families
Austin's explosive growth is creating a rapidly expanding senior care market. Adult children in Austin tend to be younger, tech-savvy decision-makers who research extensively online before contacting a facility. Strong Google and Facebook performance. CPL runs $45–$90.

Texas HHSC licensing — what families check

Texas assisted living facilities are licensed and regulated by the Texas Health and Human Services Commission (HHSC). Families in Texas increasingly research HHSC inspection records and deficiency reports before making contact with a facility — a pattern that has accelerated as consumer awareness of senior care quality standards has grown.

We track HHSC compliance records alongside CMS ratings for facilities in your competitive market. When a competitor receives a deficiency citation, we update your campaigns to lead with your clean compliance record as a differentiator. When your own licensing data shows improvement, we build that into your messaging immediately.

Texas-specific opportunity: The Texas senior care market has a significant supply gap opening in smaller metros — Lubbock, Amarillo, Corpus Christi, El Paso, and the Rio Grande Valley all have strong and growing senior populations with far less digital competition than the four major metros. These markets represent the best CPL efficiency in the state right now.

Medicare Advantage in Texas

Texas has strong MA penetration with major plans including UnitedHealthcare, Humana, Aetna/CVS, and Molina Healthcare all operating statewide. The managed Medicaid landscape in Texas — through STAR+PLUS — also generates placement referrals for assisted living and home health operators who build relationships with managed care organization case managers.

Texas assisted living marketing — FAQs

Which Texas city has the best ROI for assisted living marketing?

San Antonio and Austin currently offer the best cost-per-lead efficiency for assisted living marketing in Texas. Houston and Dallas generate higher lead volume but at higher CPL. For operators focused on maximizing ROI, San Antonio's combination of lower CPL and strong MA referral networks makes it the most efficient market in the state right now.

Do Texas assisted living facilities need bilingual marketing?

In Houston, San Antonio, El Paso, and the Rio Grande Valley — yes, bilingual marketing is essential. Spanish-speaking families represent a significant portion of the decision-making audience in these markets. Facebook campaigns in Spanish targeting adult children of Spanish-speaking seniors consistently outperform English-only campaigns in South and West Texas markets.

How does Texas HHSC licensing affect marketing?

Texas families increasingly research HHSC inspection records before contacting a facility. A clean compliance history is a genuine marketing asset in Texas. Facilities with no deficiency citations in their last two inspections convert leads at significantly higher rates than facilities with citations on record. We build your compliance record into your marketing messaging where appropriate.
