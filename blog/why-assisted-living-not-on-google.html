Why Your Assisted Living Facility Isn't Showing on Google

CareFlow Leads April 13, 2025 8 min read

82% of families research senior care facilities online before making a single phone call. If your facility isn't visible when they search, you don't exist to them. Here are the five most common reasons assisted living facilities are invisible on Google — and the exact fix for each one.

82%
Of families begin their senior care search online. The facility that appears first in Google search results for "assisted living [your city]" receives a disproportionate share of inquiries — studies consistently show the top three results capture 60–75% of all clicks.
Reason 01
Your Google Business Profile is incomplete or unclaimed

Google Business Profile is the single highest-impact free tool available to any local business — and the majority of assisted living facilities have either never claimed their profile or have left it substantially incomplete. When families search "assisted living near me," Google's local pack — the map results at the top of the page — is determined almost entirely by Google Business Profile data. If yours is incomplete, you don't appear.

Go to business.google.com right now and check whether your facility's profile is claimed. If it isn't, claim it. If it is claimed, check that every field is complete: hours, services offered, description, photos, and categories. Facilities categorized as "Assisted Living Facility" consistently appear in more senior care searches than those generically categorized as "Nursing Home" or "Healthcare."

The Fix

Claim and fully complete your Google Business Profile. Add at minimum 15 photos showing your exterior, interior, dining area, activities, and staff. Set your primary category to "Assisted Living Facility." Add a 750-character description naturally using your city name and care types. This alone can generate 5–15 additional inquiry calls per month within 4–8 weeks.

Reason 02
Your website doesn't mention your city or neighborhood

This is the most common technical SEO failure in senior care. A facility's website might be beautifully designed with detailed information about care philosophy, amenities, and staff — but if it never mentions the city it's located in, Google has no way to know it's relevant to families searching in that area.

Google matches search queries to pages based on relevance signals. "Assisted living near me" is a local search — Google returns results it believes are physically nearby and relevant. If your website says "We provide compassionate memory care" but never says "in Tampa, Florida," you're invisible for every local search in Tampa.

The Fix

Add your city, state, and neighborhood to your homepage title tag, meta description, H1 heading, and at least three places in your body copy. Create location-specific pages for every care type you offer: "Assisted Living in [City]," "Memory Care in [City]," "Home Health in [City]." Each page should be 600–1,000 words and mention the city naturally throughout.

Reason 03
You have no Google reviews — or too few to rank

Google uses review volume and rating as a significant local ranking factor. A facility with 8 reviews will consistently rank below a competitor with 65 reviews, even if the underlying care quality is equivalent. Reviews serve two functions: they signal credibility to Google's algorithm, and they directly influence whether families click on your listing once they see it.

The benchmark for strong local visibility is 50+ reviews. Facilities with fewer than 20 reviews are significantly disadvantaged in local search rankings. Facilities with more than 100 reviews and a 4.5+ average rating dominate their local market in Google results.

The Fix

Set a goal of one new Google review per week. After every positive family interaction — a tour, a move-in, a compliment at a care conference — send a direct review link within 24 hours via text message: "Hi [name], it was wonderful meeting you today. If you have a moment, we'd be so grateful for a Google review — it helps other families find us." One review per week compounds to 52 reviews in a year.

Reason 04
Your website has no content Google can rank

Most assisted living websites are essentially digital brochures — a homepage, an about page, a services page, and a contact form. This structure gives Google almost nothing to rank for beyond your facility name and city. Families searching for answers to questions like "how to choose a memory care facility" or "signs a parent needs assisted living" will never find you because you haven't written anything addressing those questions.

Content — specifically articles that answer the questions families actually search — is the primary driver of organic search visibility for senior care facilities that rank well. The facilities that dominate organic search have 20–50 articles on their websites covering every question a family might ask during the 6–18 month decision process.

The Fix

Start a blog and publish one article per month targeting a specific family question. Good starting topics: "How to talk to a parent about assisted living," "What does assisted living cost in [state]," "The difference between assisted living and memory care," "Signs your parent needs more care than you can provide at home." Each article builds authority and creates new ranking opportunities for your domain.

Reason 05
No other websites link to yours

Google interprets links from other websites to your website as votes of credibility — the more high-quality sites that link to you, the more Google trusts your site and the higher it ranks. A newly launched facility website with no external links will struggle to rank even with perfect content and technical SEO, because Google has no external signal that the site is trustworthy or authoritative.

For senior care specifically, the most valuable links come from healthcare industry directories, local business associations, state senior care associations, and local news coverage. These are not difficult to obtain — they simply require intentional outreach.

The Fix

Get listed on: your state's senior care association directory, your local Chamber of Commerce website, LeadingAge.org, Argentum.org, and at least three local healthcare provider directories. Each listing creates a backlink to your site. Submit a brief article to a local news outlet about a community event at your facility — local news coverage generates high-value backlinks and is easier to obtain than most operators realize.

The compound effect: Fix all five of these issues and you won't see results tomorrow. You will see them in 90–180 days. SEO is not instant — but the operators who start now will own their local search results in six months while competitors who wait will still be invisible.

Frequently asked questions

How long does it take for an assisted living facility to rank on Google?

A new or newly optimized website typically takes 3–6 months to begin ranking for local assisted living keywords and 6–12 months to achieve page one rankings for competitive terms. Local SEO through Google Business Profile can generate visibility much faster — often within 4–8 weeks of optimization.

What keywords should an assisted living facility target?

Start with location-specific keywords like "assisted living [your city]", "memory care [your city]", and "senior care near me." Also target cost and comparison keywords like "how much does assisted living cost in [state]" and condition-specific terms like "Alzheimer's care [city]." These mid-funnel keywords have strong intent and lower competition than generic terms.

Does Google Business Profile help assisted living facilities rank?

Yes, significantly. A fully optimized Google Business Profile is one of the fastest ways to gain local search visibility for an assisted living facility. It enables you to appear in the Google Maps pack — the three listings that appear above organic results for local searches — which receives a disproportionate share of clicks from families searching for nearby facilities.
