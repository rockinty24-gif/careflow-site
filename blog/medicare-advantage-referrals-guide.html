Medicare Advantage Referrals for Assisted Living: A Complete Guide

CareFlow Leads April 13, 2025 9 min read

Medicare Advantage care coordinators are the single most underutilized referral source in senior care. They convert at 60–80%, cost nothing per referral once relationships are built, and send families who are already decided and ready to move. This guide shows you exactly how to build and maintain these relationships.

70%
Average conversion rate for Medicare Advantage referrals — compared to 15–25% for cold digital leads. A family referred by an MA care coordinator has already been assessed, is eligible for placement, and trusts the professional who recommended your facility.

What Medicare Advantage care coordinators actually do

A Medicare Advantage care coordinator is a healthcare professional — typically a registered nurse, social worker, or certified case manager — employed by an MA insurance plan. Their job is to help plan members navigate complex care transitions, including assisted living placement, memory care, home health, and skilled nursing.

When a member's health status changes — a hospitalization, a fall, a dementia diagnosis — the care coordinator steps in to help the family understand their options and navigate the placement process. They maintain relationships with facilities in their coverage area and refer families to the ones they know, trust, and have had good experiences with.

There are currently over 3,000 Medicare Advantage organizations operating in the United States, covering more than 33 million Medicare-eligible Americans. In most mid-size metro areas, 5–15 MA plans are actively operating and each has dedicated care coordinators managing member transitions.

Why most facilities miss this channel entirely

The reason MA referrals are underutilized is simple: they require relationship investment rather than ad spend. Most operators are conditioned to think about marketing in terms of budgets and campaigns. MA referral development is more like business development — it's about people, trust, and consistent presence over time.

The facilities that dominate MA referral volume in any market are not necessarily the largest or most expensive. They are the ones that show up consistently, make the coordinator's job easier, and give families an exceptional experience so the coordinator never has to worry about a referral going badly.

The key insight: A care coordinator who refers a family to your facility is putting their professional reputation on the line. Make that referral the easiest, smoothest experience possible — for both the coordinator and the family — and you become their default recommendation for every eligible placement in your service area.

How to find every MA plan in your market

Before you can build relationships, you need to know who to contact. Here is the exact process to map every MA plan operating in your area:

  1. Go to medicare.gov → Plan Finder → enter your zip code → filter by Medicare Advantage plans. This shows every plan covering your geography.
  2. For each plan, visit their website and look for "Provider Relations," "Care Management," or "Case Management" contact information.
  3. Call the member services number for each plan and ask to be connected with the care coordination or care management team — specifically the team handling long-term care placements.
  4. Build a contact spreadsheet with: plan name, care coordinator name, phone, email, territory covered, and monthly follow-up date.

In most markets you will identify 6–15 MA plans operating in your service area. Each plan has multiple care coordinators depending on their member volume. Your goal in the first 90 days is to have an introductory conversation with at least one coordinator from each plan.

The 6-step relationship building system

1

The introduction call

Call each plan's care management team and introduce yourself and your facility. Keep it simple and professional — you are not pitching, you are introducing a resource. Your goal is to schedule a facility tour.

2

The facility tour

Invite the coordinator for a 30-minute tour. Show them the care environment, introduce them to your clinical team, and give them a clear picture of your care levels, specializations, and typical resident profile. Make it easy for them to visualize placing a member with you.

3

The availability sheet

Create a simple one-page document showing your current availability by care level, your pricing range, and your intake process. Send this by email after every tour and update it monthly. Coordinators who know you have availability and know your process will think of you first when a placement need arises.

4

The monthly check-in

Once a relationship is established, a brief monthly email — 3–4 sentences maximum — maintaining the connection and sharing any updates on availability or new care capabilities is all you need. Consistency over time is what converts a cold contact into a reliable referral source.

5

The exceptional intake experience

When a coordinator sends you a referral, the experience you provide that family directly determines whether you get the next referral. Respond within 60 minutes. Have a dedicated intake coordinator reach out personally. Make the tour and paperwork process seamless. Follow up with the coordinator after move-in.

6

The quarterly in-person visit

Four times per year, visit your top-referring coordinators in person — bringing a small appreciation gift and spending 10 minutes catching up. These visits reinforce the personal relationship that makes you their top recommendation over facilities they only know by email.

What to say on the first call

Introduction call script

"Hi, my name is [name], and I'm the [title] at [facility name] in [city]. We're a [X]-bed [care type] facility serving [area]. I'm reaching out because I want to make sure your care coordination team knows about us as a placement resource for your members. We specialize in [specific care strengths], and we currently have [X] openings available. Would it be possible to schedule a brief 30-minute tour so your team can see our environment and we can talk about how we can best serve your members?"

A realistic timeline: Month 1–2: introduce yourself to all MA plans in your market. Month 2–3: complete tours with care coordinators from 4–6 plans. Month 3–4: first referrals begin arriving from relationships you built earliest. Month 6+: steady referral flow from 3–5 active MA relationships generating 3–8 referrals per month at zero cost per lead.

Frequently asked questions

What is a Medicare Advantage care coordinator?

A Medicare Advantage care coordinator is a healthcare professional employed by an MA insurance plan who helps eligible members navigate care transitions, including placement in assisted living, memory care, or skilled nursing facilities. They are one of the most valuable referral sources for senior care operators because they work directly with families at the moment a placement decision is being made.

How do I find Medicare Advantage plans operating in my market?

Go to Medicare.gov and use the Plan Finder tool to identify every MA plan operating in your county. Then contact each plan directly through their provider relations department and ask to be connected with their care coordination team. The CMS website also publishes a complete list of all MA organizations by state.

How long does it take to start receiving MA referrals?

Building MA referral relationships typically takes 3–6 months before you see consistent referral volume. The first 60–90 days involve introducing your facility, scheduling tours, and getting on their preferred provider list. Regular monthly touchpoints after that build the relationship to the point where coordinators think of your facility first when a placement need arises.
