How Much Does Assisted Living Marketing Cost in 2025?

CareFlow Leads April 13, 2025 7 min read

Most senior care operators know they need to market their facility — but almost none have a clear picture of what each channel actually costs and what they should expect in return. This guide breaks down every channel with real cost-per-lead benchmarks so you can budget with confidence.

20x
Average annual return on a well-run senior care marketing program. At $80,000 average annual revenue per resident, adding three residents per month against a $12,000/month marketing investment generates a 20x annual return. The math almost always works — if the system is built correctly.

The honest truth about marketing costs

The single biggest mistake operators make is evaluating marketing cost in isolation — looking at the monthly spend without connecting it to the revenue each new resident generates. A $10,000/month marketing program sounds expensive until you realize one new resident at $80,000 annual revenue pays for the entire year in 45 days.

The second mistake is chasing the cheapest cost per lead. A $20 lead that converts at 2% is dramatically more expensive than a $120 lead that converts at 35%. Medicare Advantage referrals cost almost nothing per lead and convert at 60–80%. The cost efficiency calculation always needs to include conversion rate, not just acquisition cost.

Google Ads: what you're actually paying for

Google Search Ads for senior care keywords cost $8–$18 per click in most markets. At an average landing page conversion rate of 8–12%, that translates to $67–$225 per lead. The wide range comes down entirely to how well the campaign is structured — the right keywords, the right negative keywords, and dedicated landing pages per care type consistently produce leads at $40–$80.

Google Local Services Ads work differently — you pay per phone call, not per click. The cost per call ranges from $30–$100 depending on your market and your review volume. Facilities with 50+ Google reviews consistently pay less per call and appear higher in LSA rankings. If you have fewer than 20 reviews, getting to 50 is the single highest-ROI thing you can do before launching Google Ads.

The review strategy most operators miss: Every time a family member expresses satisfaction — in person, by email, or on the phone — send them a direct Google review link within 24 hours. Set a goal of one new review per week. A facility with 80 reviews pays 30–40% less per Google LSA lead than a facility with 15 reviews.

Facebook and Instagram: who it reaches and what it costs

Facebook's targeting for senior care is genuinely powerful. Adult children aged 45–65 within a specific radius, filtered by caregiving interests, AARP membership signals, and life events like "recently bereaved" or "caring for aging parent" — this audience is actively thinking about senior care and hasn't found your facility yet.

The cost per lead on Facebook ranges from $25–$80 depending on your market size and creative quality. Video ads showing actual facility tours and staff introductions consistently outperform static images by 2–3x. A 60-second virtual tour video filmed on an iPhone with good lighting will outperform a professionally designed graphic ad almost every time in this market.

Why Medicare Advantage referrals are the best value in senior care

MA care coordinators place thousands of eligible seniors into facilities annually — and they cost you nothing per referral once the relationship is established. The investment is time, not money: monthly check-ins, quarterly facility tours, and keeping coordinators updated on your current availability and care capabilities.

These referrals convert at 60–80% because the family already trusts the care coordinator who recommended you. Compare that to a cold digital lead converting at 15–25% and the value difference is stark. A facility generating 5 MA referrals per month at 70% conversion is adding 3–4 new residents monthly at zero ad cost.

What a realistic monthly budget looks like

Small facility (under 40 beds)

Start with Google LSA ($1,500/month) and one Facebook retargeting campaign ($500/month). Total: $2,000/month. At current CPL benchmarks, expect 15–25 qualified inquiries per month. Adding two residents at this budget level generates a 13x monthly return.

Mid-size facility (40–80 beds)

Google LSA plus Google Search Ads plus Facebook ($5,000–$8,000/month). This is the sweet spot for most operators — enough budget to run all high-intent channels simultaneously. Expect 25–45 qualified inquiries per month. A fully managed program at this level typically runs $10,000–$12,000/month including management fees.

Regional operator (multiple facilities)

Full multi-channel program including Google, Facebook, YouTube retargeting, and MA referral network management. Budget $20,000–$40,000/month across facilities. Dedicated campaign manager per division. Quarterly strategy reviews. This level generates 60–120+ qualified inquiries monthly across your portfolio.

The number to remember: Your marketing budget should never exceed the monthly revenue from one new resident. At $80,000 annual revenue per resident, that is $6,667 per month. If your program adds even one resident monthly, it pays for itself. If it adds three, your annual ROI is 20x.

Frequently asked questions

How much does Google Ads cost for assisted living?

Google Ads for assisted living typically costs $40–$120 per lead with a monthly budget of $3,000–$8,000 for a single facility. Highly competitive markets like Florida, California, and Texas trend toward the higher end. Google Local Services Ads are more efficient at $30–$100 per qualified call.

Is Facebook advertising effective for senior care?

Yes. Facebook and Instagram ads targeting adult children aged 45–65 generate leads at $25–$80 each for assisted living facilities. The key is targeting by caregiving interests and life events rather than broad demographics. Video tour ads and staff spotlights consistently outperform static image ads.

What is a reasonable assisted living marketing budget?

A practical benchmark is to budget no more than the monthly revenue from one new resident. At $80,000 average annual revenue per resident, that is roughly $6,700 per month. Most operators running effective multi-channel programs spend $5,000–$15,000 per month and see 15–30x annual returns.
