The Florida senior care market — what operators need to know

Florida is the largest senior care market in the United States by facility count and one of the most competitive for digital marketing. With 21% of the state's population over 65 and an estimated 1,000 new seniors arriving in Florida every day, demand is enormous — but so is the competition for their attention online.

The facilities winning in Florida's digital landscape share three characteristics: strong Google Business Profiles with 50+ reviews, active Google Local Services Ads campaigns, and warm Medicare Advantage referral relationships with the major MA plans operating across South Florida, the I-4 corridor, and the Gulf Coast markets.

Florida CMS ratings — why they matter for your marketing

Florida's Agency for Health Care Administration (AHCA) inspects and licenses all assisted living facilities in the state. Families searching for Florida facilities cross-reference AHCA records and Medicare's Care Compare ratings before making contact. A facility with a strong rating and visible online presence converts leads at nearly twice the rate of a facility with a weak rating and poor visibility.

We monitor AHCA licensing data and CMS Care Compare ratings for every facility in your competitive market weekly. When a competitor's rating drops, we shift your campaigns to capture families who were considering that facility. When your own rating improves, we update your messaging immediately to lead with that credibility signal.

Florida-specific opportunity: Florida has more 1 and 2-star CMS-rated facilities than almost any other state. Families who discover a competitor's poor rating mid-search are actively looking for an alternative. Our CMS monitoring system identifies these moments in real time and routes those families to your facility.

Medicare Advantage in Florida — the referral opportunity

Florida has one of the highest Medicare Advantage penetration rates in the country — over 55% of Florida Medicare beneficiaries are enrolled in an MA plan. That means the majority of seniors transitioning into assisted living in Florida are MA members, and their care coordinators are actively placing them into facilities across the state.

The major MA plans operating in Florida include UnitedHealthcare, Humana, Simply Healthcare (Wellcare), Devoted Health, and Florida Blue. Each plan has dedicated care coordinators managing member placements. Building relationships with even two or three of these plans can generate 5–10 warm referrals per month at zero cost per lead.

  • Google LSA setup and management for every Florida market — including the AHCA license verification Google requires for healthcare providers

  • CMS and AHCA monitoring for your competitive market updated weekly

Florida assisted living marketing — FAQs

How much does assisted living marketing cost in Florida?

Assisted living marketing in Florida typically costs $5,000–$15,000 per month for a fully managed multi-channel program. Google Ads CPL runs $50–$140 in competitive markets like Miami and Orlando. At $80,000 average annual revenue per resident, adding two residents monthly generates a 15–20x annual return on investment.

Which Florida markets are most competitive for assisted living?

Miami-Dade, Broward, and Palm Beach counties are the most competitive markets in Florida for assisted living, with the highest concentration of facilities and Google Ads costs. Orlando, Tampa, and Jacksonville offer better cost-per-lead efficiency with strong family search volume. Retirement-heavy markets like Sarasota, Naples, and The Villages have high demand and relatively low digital competition.

How do Florida CMS ratings affect assisted living marketing?

Florida has over 3,700 licensed assisted living facilities, making CMS star ratings a critical differentiator. Families searching online frequently check Care Compare ratings before contacting a facility. Facilities with 4–5 star ratings in Florida convert leads at 35–45% compared to 20–25% for 3-star facilities. Our CMS monitoring system tracks rating changes across your Florida market weekly.
